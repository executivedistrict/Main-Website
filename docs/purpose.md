# Purpose - why this site exists

> **Read this before doing any development.** Every design, copy, and structural
> decision on this site serves the purpose below. If a change doesn't advance it,
> it doesn't belong. For who the company is, see
> [`company-background.md`](company-background.md).

## The mandate

This is a **deliberate rebuild**, not a refresh. The founder, **Zach Booker**,
was ready to **shut the old site down** - and the reason matters: **lead quality
wasn't good enough.** The pattern he described:

- People booked calls and **didn't show up** (no-shows), or
- They showed up but were **the wrong fit**.

So the old site generated *traffic and bookings*, but not *the right
conversations*. We are rebuilding to fix exactly that.

## The single goal: a lead-qualification tool

This site's job is **not** to maximize visitors or even bookings. Its job is to
**improve the quality of who reaches the calendar.** Success is measured by:

- **Show rate** - booked calls that actually happen.
- **Fit rate** - calls that are with the right kind of owner.
- Fewer, better conversations - not more of them.

**Qualifying the right people in and keeping the wrong people out are equally
important.** A great outcome is a wrong-fit visitor reading the site and
*deciding not to book.* Repelling the wrong lead is a win, not a loss. Design and
copy should make the right person feel "this is for me" and make the wrong person
feel "this isn't for me" - on purpose.

## Who we are targeting

The primary target market (per Zach, based in **West Michigan**):

- **Older, boomer-generation business owners** - founders/owners who have run
  their company for decades.
- At a **sell-or-transition** moment: ready to **sell, exit, or move the
  business into its next stage** (succession, stepping back, life after the
  business). Tired of carrying it alone; thinking about legacy and what's next.
- Local / regional **West Michigan** owners are the core, not a national
  tech-startup audience.

This narrows the broad "$500K–$50M, grow/stabilize/exit" positioning of the old
site toward the **transition/exit** end of that range, for an **older
demographic.** Lead with that.

## Who we are NOT for (state it plainly)

- Founders just looking for free advice, coaching, or a "pick your brain" call.
- Tire-kickers who book to browse with no intent to act.
- Companies (or people) outside the fit - too early, not serious about a real
  engagement, or not actually contemplating a transition.

Making this explicit on the site is part of the qualification - it's how the
wrong people self-select out before they ever hit the calendar.

## How to manage the traffic (operating principles)

Apply these to every page, component, and copy decision:

1. **Qualify and disqualify on every page.** Speak so specifically to the target
   owner that the wrong visitor recognizes it's not for them. Don't write to
   "everyone."
2. **Design for the audience.** Boomer-generation owners reward clarity over
   cleverness: generous type sizes, high contrast, plain language (no startup
   jargon or trendy/hipster aesthetics), obvious navigation, fast load, strong
   mobile **and** desktop legibility, visible phone/contact options, and
   real trust signals (the operators' track record, plain-spoken proof).
3. **Pre-qualify before the calendar - this is the fix for no-shows and bad
   fit.** Don't send raw traffic straight to a booking widget. Put qualifying
   friction in front of it: a few screening questions (revenue range, role/
   ownership, where they are in the sell/transition journey, what they actually
   want), clear expectations for the call, and a commitment signal. The booking
   step should feel earned, serious, and confidential - not casual.
4. **Lead with transition/exit intent.** Center the messaging on selling,
   succession, stepping back, and what's next - the things the target owner is
   actually weighing - rather than generic "scale your business" growth copy.
5. **Use seriousness as a filter.** Confidence, specificity, and a senior
   peer-to-peer tone repel tire-kickers and attract serious owners. Set the bar
   high enough that only the right person leans in.
6. **Optimize for fit, not volume.** When a tradeoff appears between "more
   leads" and "better leads," choose better. Never add a pattern whose only
   effect is to inflate top-of-funnel numbers.

## North star

> The wrong owner reads this site and decides not to book. The right owner -
> an older West Michigan business owner ready to sell or transition - reads it,
> feels understood, and books a call they actually show up to.
